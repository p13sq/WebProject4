Chapter 2-The Council with the Munchkins

Dorothy Arrives in the Land of Oz

She was awakened by a shock, so sudden and severe that if Dorothy had not been lying on the soft bed she might have been hurt...

Dorothy sat up and noticed that the house was not moving; nor was it dark, for the bright sunshine came in at the window...

The cyclone had set the house down very gentlyâ€”in the midst of a country of marvelous beauty...

While she stood looking eagerly at the strange and beautiful sights, she noticed coming toward her a group of the queerest people she had ever seen...

The little old woman walked up to Dorothy, made a low bow and said, in a sweet voice:

You are welcome, most noble Sorceress, to the land of the Munchkins..."

"You are very kind, but there must be some mistake. I have not killed anything," said Dorothy.

"Your house did, anyway," replied the little old woman...

Dorothy looked, and gave a little cry of fright. There, indeed, just under the corner of the house, two feet were sticking out...

"She was the Wicked Witch of the East," said the little woman...

"Are you a real witch?" asked Dorothy.

Yes, indeed," answered the little woman. "But I am a good witch..."

The little old woman continued explaining about the Land of Oz and its inhabitants...

The feet of the dead Witch had disappeared entirely, and nothing was left but the silver shoes...

"LET DOROTHY GO TO THE CITY OF EMERALDS" was written on the slate.

"Then you must go to the City of Emeralds. Perhaps Oz will help you," said the Witch of the North.

"How can I get there?" asked Dorothy.

"You must walk... The road to the City of Emeralds is paved with yellow brick, so you cannot miss it..."

The Witch kissed Dorothy on the forehead and gave her a mark of protection...

The Witch and the Munchkins departed, and Dorothy, with Toto, began her journey on the yellow brick road...
